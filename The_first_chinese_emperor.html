Qin Shi Huang (c. 259 BCE - 210BCE)
Emperor of Qin Dynasty

Also known As Ying Zheng; Zheng, the King of Qin; Shi Huangdi.

The exact date of birth unknown, probably around 259 BCE in Hanan.

A tyrannical ruler who reigned China from 221 to 210 BC as the first emperor of the Qin dynasty.

Conquered the other Warring States and unified China but collapsed less than four years after his death.

Reorganized the bureaucracy, abolishing the existing nobility and replacing them with his appointed officials.

Famous for creating the magnificent and enormous construction projects such as the beginings of the Great Wall of China.

Simplified the written Chinese script, standardized weights and measures, and minted new copper coins.
